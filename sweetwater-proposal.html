02 — Scope of Work

General Requirements

InStyle Cleaning acknowledges and agrees to the following general requirements as specified by Sweetwater Gainesville management.

👷 Supervision & Compliance

  • Full-time on-site supervision provided for the entire duration of the project
  • All work performed in compliance with City, County, State, and Federal codes, ordinances, laws, and regulations
  • All unit cleaning performed to industry standards and manufacturer's specifications, including all labor and materials
  • Contractor representative on-site on Move-In Day to assist with any issues

🔄 Go-Backs & Callbacks

  • Units not meeting property quality inspection will be re-cleaned at no cost to the property
  • Minor scope changes may occur prior to actual turn date — finalized list provided by Manager before start

🏠 Partially Occupied Units

  • Some assigned units may be partially occupied
  • Occupied "common" spaces receive the same level of service as fully vacant spaces
  • Resident's possessions will not be disturbed

⚠ AC Temperature Policy

AC temperature shall not be set below 72°F at any time. Upon leaving a unit, AC must be set to 78°F with fan on AUTO. Settings below 72°F may result in a $10/day per unit deduction. Contractor acknowledges and will strictly enforce this policy with all crew members.

Property Rules & Conduct

🚫 Prohibited On-Site

  • No smoking in any units
  • No loud music (radios, boom boxes, etc.)
  • No animals allowed on-site
  • No alcohol on-site
  • No disposing of debris in unit trash
  • Unit bathrooms are off-limits for contractor use — alternative facilities provided by property
  • No use of any appliances or furniture in units

🔑 Keys, Items & Liability

  • Contractor responsible for cost of re-keying due to lost keys
  • All property keys must be returned daily before departure
  • Items found in vacant units shall be presented to the General Manager — not removed from property
  • Contractor is responsible for their employees at all times
  • Units with extensive pet hair/odor must be reported to Property Manager immediately
  • Appliances requiring help to move — site team must be notified

✦ Invoice Policy

Contractor invoice shall not exceed contract price. Any amount invoiced different from the contract shall have an approved change order attached, signed by Manager.

02 — Scope of Work (continued)

General Unit Cleaning Detail

All spaces within units — kitchens, bathrooms, living rooms, bedrooms, closets, all appliances — cleaned inside and out, free of all foreign matter.

✨ All-Unit Cleaning Standards

  • Wipe down and clean all surfaces with all-purpose cleaner to remove all foreign matter, dirt, handprints, stickers, labels, stamps, grease, and other markings
  • Detail vacuum all carpet, tile, vinyl, and similar floor areas
  • Detail clean ceramic tile and vinyl flooring with all-purpose cleaner, rinse with clean water, and wipe down with clean rags
  • Clean all windows, sliding glass doors, window & door tracks, sills, registers, and vents — inside and outside — including removal of paint, plaster, and debris
  • Vertical and mini blinds cleaned thoroughly
  • All unit entry doors, interior doors, windows, receptacles, light switches, covers, closet rods & shelves cleaned; all cobwebs removed
  • All light fixtures removed and cleaned, including entry lights and patio lights
  • Patio and storage areas wiped down and swept
  • All unit trim and baseboards wiped down

🪑 Furnished Units

  • All furniture dusted, wiped out, and cleaned underneath and behind
  • All couch cushions removed, vacuumed under, and replaced
  • All furniture surfaces cleaned thoroughly

🔧 Appliances

  • All appliances pulled out and cleaned behind/next to
  • Notify site team if help required to move appliances
  • All drip pans, microwave vents, and returns removed, cleaned, and replaced
  • Damaged drip pans reported to Property Manager for replacement

Room-Specific Standards

Detailed cleaning specifications by room type, per Sweetwater Gainesville requirements.

🍳 Kitchen — Detailed Scope

  • Clean and wipe down all kitchen areas & cabinets inside and outside with all-purpose cleaner to remove foreign matter, dirt, grease, handprints, stickers, labels, and stamps
  • Remove water scale and deposits from kitchen sink and around faucet — all edges and all sides
  • Clean light fixtures inside and outside
  • Pull out stove and clean behind & underneath; clean all stove surfaces — front, rear, sides, doors; clean all storage drawers, drip pans, broiler pans, oven racks; wipe off range elements; pull all knobs and clean behind; ranges and range pans cleaned underneath, inside and out, free of all foreign matter and grease. After cleaning, all burners turned on to burn off residual cleaning solution
  • Range hood cleaned, wiped down, and free of all grease and foreign matter
  • Stainless steel polished in all applicable areas
  • Deep clean inside of stove including cooking racks
  • Dishwasher inside and outside cleaned and wiped down; all labels and stickers removed
  • Pull out refrigerator and clean behind; pull and clean bottom kick plate and underneath; clean all refrigerator & freezer surfaces — front, rear, sides, doors; clean all storage drawers, shelves, racks, rubber seals, gaskets, ice cube trays, ice buckets; refrigerator cleaned inside and out, free of all food
  • Floor cleaned with all-purpose cleaner, rinsed with clean water, and wiped down with clean rags

🚿 Bathrooms — Detailed Scope

  • Clean and wipe down all bathroom areas inside and outside with all-purpose cleaners to remove all foreign matter, handprints, stickers, and labels
  • Clean toilets inside and outside with general non-acid bowl cleaner
  • Towel bars and paper holders cleaned and polished
  • Shower knobs and showerhead cleaned and wiped
  • Fans and fan covers removed and cleaned
  • Bathroom floor, bathtub, and tub surrounds cleaned with all-purpose cleaner, rinsed with clean water, and wiped down with clean rags. Special attention to fiberglass fixtures — no abrasive cleaning agents allowed
  • Glass shower doors spotless at end of cleaning; buffer may be used after reviewing scope with Property Manager
  • Shower floors spotless under standard cleaning
  • Caulking — remove all mildew and growth in bathroom/kitchen areas; notify management once removed for maintenance follow-up caulking
  • Light fixtures wiped down
  • Faucets cleaned with non-acid water deposit remover
  • Mirrors and medicine cabinets cleaned including removal of paint, plaster, etc.
  • Vanity/counters cleaned and wiped down; cabinet and drawer insides wiped out
