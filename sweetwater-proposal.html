Scope of Work — General Requirements

Operational standards and on-site conduct requirements

On-Site Management & Conduct

  • Vendor shall supply full time on-site supervision for the duration of the project
  • Vendor shall perform all work in compliance with all codes, ordinances, laws and regulations
  • Vendor shall perform all Unit Cleaning to industry standards and meet Manufacturer specs
  • Go-backs at no cost to property
  • No Smoking, no loud music, no animals, no alcohol on-site
  • Unit bathrooms off limits to workers

Occupied & Partially Occupied Units

  • Some units partially occupied — common spaces get same service level, resident possessions not disturbed
  • Items found in vacant units go to General Manager, not removed from property

Accountability & Penalties

  • AC not less than 72 degrees, turn to 78 when leaving. Below 72 = $10/day/unit penalty
  • Contractor responsible for re-keying costs if keys lost. Keys returned daily
  • Failure to meet completion timeframe = Contractor reimburses Owner for completion by others
  • No disposing of debris in unit trash
  • Minor changes may occur before turn date due to student housing nature

AC Compliance: InStyle Cleaning acknowledges the thermostat requirement. All units will be set to no less than 72°F during service and adjusted to 78°F upon departure. Our supervisors will verify compliance on each unit check-out.

Scope of Work — General Detail Cleaning

Full-unit cleaning standards applicable to all spaces

Surfaces, Floors & General

  • All spaces cleaned inside and out, free of foreign matter, dirt, handprints, stickers, grease
  • Detail vacuum all carpet, tile, vinyl
  • Clean ceramic tile/vinyl with all-purpose cleaner, rinse with clean water, wipe with clean rags
  • Clean all windows, sliding glass doors, tracks, sills, registers, vents inside and outside
  • Vertical and mini blinds cleaned
  • All doors, windows, receptacles, light switches, covers, closet rods/shelves cleaned. Remove cobwebs
  • Light fixtures removed and cleaned including entry and patio lights
  • Patio and storage areas wiped down and swept
  • Wipe down all trim/baseboards

Furnished Units & Appliances

  • Furnished homes: dust, wipe, clean underneath/behind all furniture. Vacuum under couch cushions
  • Pet hair/odor units: notify Property Manager immediately
  • All appliances pulled out and cleaned behind/next to
  • Drip pans, microwave vents, returns removed, cleaned, replaced. Notify if damaged

Administrative

  • Invoice shall not exceed contract price. Change orders must be approved and signed by Manager
  • Representative on-site on Move-In Day
  • Minor changes may occur before turn date due to student housing nature

Scope of Work — Kitchen & Bathrooms

Detailed cleaning specifications by room type

Kitchen

  • Clean all kitchen areas and cabinets inside/outside
  • Remove water scales from sink and faucet
  • Clean light fixtures inside/outside
  • Pull out stove, clean behind/underneath, all surfaces, drip pans, broiler pans, oven racks, knobs. Burn off residual cleaning solution
  • Range hood cleaned, free of grease
  • Polish stainless steel
  • Deep clean inside stove including racks
  • Dishwasher inside/outside cleaned, labels removed
  • Pull out refrigerator, clean behind, kick plate, all surfaces, drawers, shelves, rubber seals, ice trays. Free of all food
  • Floor cleaned with all-purpose cleaner, rinsed, wiped

Bathrooms

  • Clean all bathroom areas inside/outside
  • Toilets inside/outside with non-acid bowl cleaner
  • Towel bars and paper holders cleaned and polished
  • Shower knobs and showerhead cleaned
  • Fans and fan covers removed and cleaned
  • Floor, bathtub, tub surrounds cleaned, rinsed, wiped. No abrasive cleaners on fiberglass
  • Glass shower doors spotless. Buffer approved after reviewing with property manager
  • Shower floors spotless
  • Caulking: remove mildew/growth, notify management for follow-up caulking
  • Light fixtures wiped down
  • Faucet cleaned with non-acid water deposit remover
  • Mirror and medicine cabinets cleaned
  • Vanity/counters cleaned, cabinet/drawer insides wiped

Material Safety: InStyle Cleaning uses only non-acid cleaners for toilets and faucets, and avoids abrasive products on fiberglass surfaces. All cleaning solutions are commercial-grade and EPA-compliant. MSDS sheets available upon request.

Quality Assurance Process

Our three-tier inspection system ensures zero go-backs

Step 01
Crew Self-Check
Each cleaning crew completes a 40-point checklist before leaving every unit. Items include all kitchen appliances, bathroom fixtures, floor surfaces, windows, blinds, and thermostat setting verification.
Step 02
Supervisor Walkthrough
Full-time on-site supervisor performs independent walkthrough of every completed unit using white-glove standards. Photo documentation of each unit before and after for accountability records.
Step 03
Manager Sign-Off
Joint walkthrough with Sweetwater Property Manager for final acceptance. Any deficiencies addressed immediately as go-backs at zero additional cost. Real-time communication channel for issue escalation.
Go-Back Policy
  • Cost to Property: $0 — all go-backs are at InStyle's expense
  • Response Time: Same-day turnaround for all reported items
  • Documentation: Photo evidence of resolution provided to management
  • Escalation: Direct phone line to project supervisor during entire turn
Key Management Protocol
  • Daily Return: All keys returned to property office at end of each work day
  • Tracking: Key sign-out/sign-in log maintained with supervisor signature
  • Lost Keys: InStyle assumes full responsibility for re-keying costs
  • Security: Keys stored in locked key box during working hours

Execution Plan

Phased approach to complete all 144 units on schedule

Phase 1 — Pre-Turn
Mobilization & Planning
Site walkthrough with Sweetwater management. Receive unit list with configurations and priority order. Coordinate key distribution protocol. Stage supplies and equipment at designated on-site storage area. Confirm crew assignments and shift schedules with supervisor.
Phase 2 — Execution
Unit Turnover Cleaning
Dedicated crews deployed across floors following the property's priority sequence. Each crew handles 6–8 units per day depending on configuration. Supervisor conducts real-time quality checks. Daily progress reports submitted to property management. Partially occupied units handled per special protocol — common areas serviced, resident belongings undisturbed.
Phase 3 — QA & Handoff
Quality Verification
Three-tier inspection of every completed unit. Go-backs addressed same-day. Joint walkthrough with Sweetwater team for final acceptance. Complete key return with documented sign-off. All items found in vacant units delivered to General Manager.
Phase 4 — Move-In Day
On-Site Support
InStyle representative present on Move-In Day to handle any last-minute cleaning needs or resident concerns. Direct communication line maintained with property management for rapid response.
